Day 1: Arrival & Alfama Vibes

I arrived in Lisbon on a sunny Friday afternoon, and the warmth was the perfect welcome. After checking in to my cozy Airbnb nestled in the heart of Alfama, I set out to explore the neighborhood’s cobbled streets, pastel buildings, and the distant sound of Fado music echoing through alleyways. Highlights: Miradouro de Santa Luzia – Best sunset view I’ve ever seen. Bifana sandwich from a street stall — simple but delicious. Got lost (on purpose) and stumbled upon a local art fair.

Day 2: Castles, Trams & Tarts

Started the day early with a hearty breakfast of pastéis de nata and espresso. Took the iconic Tram 28 through the city — yes, it’s touristy, but worth every bump and rattle. Visited São Jorge Castle, where I could see the entire city unfold below. In the afternoon, I wandered through the Baixa and Chiado districts for some shopping and people-watching. Top Picks: Time Out Market – Great food and lively atmosphere. LX Factory – Trendy spot full of street art, books, and cafes. Local ginja (cherry liqueur) – Strong but sweet!
